How it looks in practice

From no website to fully booked

Tristen (TatsByTris) had no web presence at all, just an Instagram. I built a dark, moody portfolio site with a live gallery and a booking form connected straight to a calendar, so new clients could find the work and book a session without a single DM.

Now it's maintained monthly: new pieces uploaded as they're finished, no extra work on their end.

About

Built by an artist, for tattoo artists

I'm Duraan, an artist myself, and the person who'll actually build and run your site. No account managers, no outsourcing. When you reach out, you're working directly with the person doing the work.

I started Easel & Ease because most working tattoo artists I know don't have a real website at all. Not because they don't want one, but because the tools (Squarespace, Wix, all of it) feel like one more job on top of their actual job. So I handle it instead.

A few things I believe

  • Your site should look like your work, not a template
  • You shouldn't have to learn web design to have a web presence
  • Maintenance shouldn't be an afterthought
